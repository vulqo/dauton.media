# Monetization Strategy

**Department:** Business
**Owner:** Luis Figuera
**Last updated:** April 22, 2026
**Status:** Planning

---

## 1. Philosophy

Culture Wiki's monetization is designed around three constraints, in order of priority:

1. **Do not compromise editorial independence.** No revenue source may influence what gets covered, how it gets covered, or which facts appear in the archive.
2. **Do not degrade user experience.** No intrusive ads, no popups, no content gates on factual data, no dark patterns.
3. **Build multiple small streams rather than one large dependency.** Concentration risk is strategic risk.

The model is deliberately modeled on The Information, 404 Media, Defector, and Aftermath — small publications that survive and thrive on reader-funded, diversified revenue rather than ad-tech dependency.

## 3. Stream detail

### 3.1 Memberships

The core revenue stream. Reader-funded, recurring, high-margin.

**Tiers:**

| Tier | Price | What you get |
|---|---|---|
| **Free** | $0 | Full archive access, editorial content, account features, favorites |
| **Supporter** | $5/month or $50/year | Early access to features, members-only newsletter, supporter badge, ad-free (if sponsorships ever show) |
| **Patron** | $15/month or $150/year | Everything above + members-only editorial (deep dives, interviews, analysis pieces), quarterly digital zine |
| **Institution** | $500/year | For libraries, universities, newsrooms — 5 seats, full archive, citation API access |

**Positioning:** Memberships are explicitly framed as supporting the work, not gating content. The archive stays free.

**Key principle:** We never put reference data behind a paywall. Factual data about artists, producers, releases, and events remains free and public forever. Only editorial deep-dives and premium features are members-only.

**Year 1 target:** 100 paid members by end of Q1 2027 ≈ $500-750 MRR.

### 3.2 Sponsorships

Long-form, transparent sponsorships — similar to podcast host-read ads or Stratechery-style sponsor posts.

**Format:**
- Dedicated sponsor week (1 sponsor per week for a specific section or piece).
- Fully disclosed with a "Sponsored by [X]" banner.
- Sponsor does not review or approve editorial content.
- We publish a public list of current and past sponsors.

**Who we'll work with:**
- Labels (for roster awareness, not for puff pieces on specific artists)
- Music-tech tools (distribution, royalty, production software)
- Streaming platforms (for playlist curation partnerships, not editorial influence)
- Cultural institutions (museums, archives, festivals)
- Brands that align culturally with Latin hip-hop (streetwear, audio gear, etc.)

**Who we won't work with:**
- Gambling, crypto speculative products, alcohol-focused campaigns targeting minors
- Any sponsor requesting editorial input, link placements, or coverage commitments
- Competitors or would-be replacements for the archive itself

**Pricing (starting estimates, to be calibrated):**
- Weekly sponsor post: $500-1,500 depending on traffic
- Newsletter sponsorship (dedicated section): $250-750

**Year 1 target:** 2-3 sponsors active by Q4 2026.

### 3.3 Newsletter sponsorships

Separate from site sponsorships, targeted at the email list specifically. Ghost/Beehiiv-style dedicated ad slot in each newsletter send.

**Pricing:** CPM-based once list is 5k+. Flat rate below that.

### 3.4 Data licensing

The most important long-term stream. The structured dataset — artist graph, release metadata, relationships — becomes licensable to:

- Academic institutions researching Latin American popular culture
- Music data companies (Chartmetric, Soundcharts, etc.) expanding Latin coverage
- Streaming platforms seeking enriched editorial metadata
- Media outlets building comparison or recommendation tools
- Labels doing catalog research

**Licensing structure:**
- Non-commercial academic: free with citation
- Commercial standard: tiered by volume of API calls or dataset size
- Enterprise: custom agreements

**This does not come online until Year 2+** when the dataset is comprehensive enough to be valuable. Year 1 is about building it.

### 3.5 Merchandise

Low-priority revenue but high-priority for community and brand identity. Tasteful, limited, well-designed physical goods.

**Initial products (Year 1-2):**
- T-shirts and hoodies with archive-themed graphics (liner notes aesthetics, typographic designs, scene-specific motifs)
- Zines or print editions of editorial collections
- Prints / posters featuring graph visualizations or editorial artwork

**Margins:** 40-60% gross on POD (print-on-demand). Not a primary revenue driver — a brand extension.

### 3.6 Events / live

Long-term stream. Panels, cypher events, documentary screenings, release parties in partnership with venues.

**Not a Year 1 priority.** Requires audience density in specific cities (likely NYC, Miami, Madrid, Caracas diaspora hubs) that we don't have yet.

### 3.7 Affiliate links

Minimal and ethical — only where they make sense and are disclosed.

- Bandcamp/Amazon/streaming links on release pages: if the user buys, small commission. Disclosed.
- Book recommendations on editorial pieces: Bookshop.org affiliate. Disclosed.
- No affiliate links on reference pages. Only in clearly editorial context.

Expected to contribute <5% of revenue.

### 3.8 What we will not do

**Display advertising (Google AdSense, programmatic ads).** Destroys UX, destroys brand, compromises SEO signals, yields pennies per visitor. Not an option.

**Sponsored reference entries.** Labels or artists paying for placement, preferential framing, or removal of unflattering facts. Disqualifying. If discovered to have happened, the person responsible is out.

**Subscriber-gated reference data.** Factual information about the scene belongs to the scene. Paywalling it destroys our authority.

**Affiliate-dependent content.** Writing articles whose primary purpose is affiliate revenue ("best headphones for hip-hop"). Destroys editorial credibility.

## 5. Pricing psychology

We price like a serious publication, not like a SaaS product or a Patreon tier:

- **Annual discount** visible ($50/year vs. $5×12 = $60).
- **No free trials** on paid tiers. Free tier is the trial — and it's generous.
- **Never discount below $5/month.** Cheapens the product and the work.
- **Grandfather early supporters.** Launch-month members lock in their rate for life.
- **Refunds on request**, no questions. One-click cancellation.

---

## 6. Anti-goals

What success does **not** look like:

- Becoming dependent on one sponsor or one customer class
- Clickbait headlines driving traffic for ad impressions
- Sacrificing archive completeness to produce viral content
- "Growth hacks" that acquire users who will never engage with the work
- Becoming a venture-scale company under pressure to 10x in 3 years

Culture Wiki is a long-term, category-defining project. The monetization model is designed to support that horizon, not optimize for a quick exit.
