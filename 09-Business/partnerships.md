# Partnerships Strategy

**Department:** Business
**Owner:** Luis Figuera
**Last updated:** April 22, 2026
**Status:** Planning

---

## 1. Philosophy

Partnerships are a force multiplier, but only when they preserve editorial independence and brand integrity. Culture Wiki will pursue partnerships that:

- **Extend reach** without compromising voice.
- **Enrich data** without creating exclusivity traps.
- **Build legitimacy** without transferring control.

The default mode is **independent and partner-aware**, not **partner-dependent**. Every partnership must survive the question: *"If this partner walked away tomorrow, would Culture Wiki still function?"* If the answer is no, the partnership is too deep.

## 2. Partnership categories

### 2.1 Editorial / media partnerships

Other Spanish-language music publications, cultural outlets, and podcasts whose audience overlaps with ours.

**What we offer:**
- Cross-promotion of editorial pieces
- Guest writing (them writing on our platform, us on theirs)
- Co-produced series (investigations, historical pieces)
- Citation exchanges (they cite us as data source, we cite them as primary reporting)

**What we ask:**
- Clear attribution
- No exclusivity
- Editorial parity (their editors don't touch our words, ours don't touch theirs)

**Potential partners (illustrative, not commitments):**
- Remezcla — broad Latin culture, strong editorial, aligned audience
- Rolling Stone en Español — institutional legitimacy, broad reach
- Indiehoy, Noisey ES archives, local music blogs in target markets
- Independent Latin rap podcasts (various — not limited to one)
- Academic journals covering Latin American popular culture

**What we avoid:**
- Exclusive content deals that lock our work to one platform
- "Official media partner" arrangements that imply endorsement
- Partnerships that require us to coordinate publishing schedules with theirs

---

### 2.2 Data & platform partnerships

Technical integrations with services that hold data useful to the archive, or vice versa.

**Inbound (us consuming):**
- Spotify for Developers — discography, audio features, artist IDs. Standard API, no deal needed.
- MusicBrainz — comprehensive credit data. Open database, we contribute back corrections.
- Genius API — lyrics and song facts.
- YouTube Data API — music videos and official uploads.
- Discogs — physical release data (vinyl, CDs) for deeper completeness.
- Wikidata / Wikipedia — editable reference nodes, we enrich and correct upstream.

**Outbound (us providing):**
- Once the archive matures (Year 2+), we offer a **citation-friendly API** to academic and journalistic users free of charge.
- Commercial data licensing to platforms seeking enriched Latin music metadata (see [`monetization.md`](./monetization.md)).

**Principles:**
- **We never lock data behind exclusive deals.** If Spotify wants enriched metadata, they can license it — but we also license to Apple, YouTube, Amazon, etc.
- **Upstream contribution is mandatory.** When we correct MusicBrainz or Wikidata, we push corrections back. The global commons benefits.

---

### 2.3 Artist & industry partnerships

Relationships with the scene itself — artists, producers, managers, labels, DJs.

**Verified profiles program:**
Artists can claim their own profile through a verification process (see [`06-Operations/moderation-workflow.md`](../06-Operations/moderation-workflow.md)). They can edit biographical fields, add links, correct credit errors. They cannot delete sourced factual information or control editorial coverage of themselves.

**What artists get:**
- A canonical, well-structured profile that ranks on search.
- Control over their bio, social links, official contact info.
- Recognition as the authoritative voice on their own discography.

**What we ask in return:**
- Respect for sourced facts they may not love.
- No pressure on editorial coverage.
- Willingness to correct our errors when we make them.

**Institutional partnerships:**
- Labels — we document their artists whether they partner with us or not. Partnership means easier access to press materials, not preferential treatment.
- Academic institutions — we work with researchers studying Latin hip-hop. Access to the dataset, collaboration on specific projects.
- Museums & archives — e.g., the Museum of the City of NY's hip-hop archive, Smithsonian, equivalents in Latin America. Mutual citation, potential digitization projects.

**What we avoid:**
- "Official artist program" that creates tiers of coverage
- Exclusive interview arrangements in exchange for editorial consideration
- Any arrangement that creates a quid pro quo on coverage

---

### 2.4 Community & scene partnerships

The less formal but often most valuable layer — relationships with the communities, Discords, subreddits, scene DJs, and organizers who know the music intimately.

**Tactics:**
- Ambassadors in key cities (not paid; recognized contributors with elevated trust)
- Invited contributor program for deep scene knowledge-holders
- Regional correspondents (informal) who flag important local developments
- Discord server or Matrix space for contributors and super-fans
- Credit and visibility for volunteer contributors who move the project forward

**Principles:**
- Volunteers contribute because they believe in the archive, not because of extrinsic rewards.
- Recognition is public and generous. Credit is non-negotiable.
- No exploitation. If someone contributes substantially, we either find a way to pay them or stop accepting free labor from them.

---

### 2.5 Commercial partnerships (sponsorships)

Covered in detail in [`monetization.md`](./monetization.md). Partnerships of this type are clearly commercial, transparent, and bounded.

**Key rule:** Every commercial partnership has a written agreement that explicitly states:
- Editorial independence is preserved.
- No coverage obligations exist.
- The partnership can be ended by either party with reasonable notice.

---

### 2.6 Institutional partnerships

Universities, cultural institutions, libraries, public archives.

**Potential forms:**
- Data donation agreements (we donate dataset snapshots for academic use)
- Digitization partnerships (we help preserve primary sources held by institutions)
- Research collaborations (they cite us, we incorporate their scholarship)
- Fellowship programs (year 3+, funded contributor positions for researchers)

**Priority institutions:**
- Universidad Central de Venezuela, USB (cultural studies departments)
- El Colegio de México, UNAM (Latin American studies)
- NYU Latinx studies programs
- Smithsonian Folkways
- Cornell Hip-Hop Collection
- University of Miami, FIU (Latin music programs)

These conversations are Year 2-3, not Year 1 priorities.

## 3. Partnership decision framework

Before entering any partnership, the following checklist must be satisfied:

- [ ] **Editorial independence preserved?** Partner cannot influence what we cover or how.
- [ ] **Brand alignment?** Partner's public positioning is compatible with ours.
- [ ] **Asymmetry check?** If the partnership ends tomorrow, can we continue? If not, too deep.
- [ ] **Written agreement?** All partnerships of material scope require written terms.
- [ ] **Disclosure clarity?** Users can understand the relationship at a glance when relevant.
- [ ] **Revenue not conditional on editorial output?** No "we pay you, you cover us" arrangements.
- [ ] **Term limit?** All partnerships have a defined duration and renewal mechanism.
- [ ] **Exit clause?** Either party can exit with reasonable notice.

If any checkbox fails, the partnership is not entered.

---

## 4. Partnership taboos

These are partnership forms Culture Wiki **does not pursue under any circumstances:**

- **Exclusive content deals** that silo our work to one platform.
- **"Official archive" positioning** for any label, platform, or publication.
- **Pay-for-play editorial** disguised as partnership.
- **Data exclusivity in exchange for cash.** Data is a commons; we license, we don't sell exclusivity.
- **Partnerships with entities under active litigation against artists in the scene we cover** — reputational contamination.
- **White-labeling** our content for another brand's use.

## 5. Year 1 partnership priorities

Given the small scope of Year 1, partnerships are deliberately limited:

**Priority:**
1. Verified artist profiles (soft launch end of Q3 2026, scaled Q4).
2. MusicBrainz / Wikidata upstream contribution (ongoing throughout year).
3. Relationship-building (not formal partnerships) with 3-5 journalists at relevant outlets.

**Not priority Year 1:**
- Commercial partnerships (handled separately as sponsorships, not relationship partnerships).
- Institutional partnerships (Year 2).
- Cross-media co-productions (Year 2).

---

## 6. Governance

- All partnerships involving money, data licensing, or editorial coordination require Luis's sign-off.
- Partnerships are logged in a single partnership registry (Notion or equivalent).
- Quarterly review of all active partnerships — still serving the mission? Still balanced? Still needed?
- Partnerships that stop serving the mission are ended, cleanly and without drama.
