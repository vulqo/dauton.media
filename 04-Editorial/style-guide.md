# Style Guide

**Department:** Editorial
**Owner:** Luis Figuera
**Last updated:** April 22, 2026

---

## Scope

This is the internal style guide for every piece of copy that appears on the site — editorial articles, bios, UI microcopy, emails, social posts. Consistency across these surfaces is how we read as a single voice.

Primary language: **Spanish** (Venezuelan default, pan-Hispanic-accessible). Anything written in English is either UI in admin (internal), or an eventual English edition (Phase 3+). This guide focuses on Spanish.

---

## Orthography and grammar

### Accents and punctuation
- Use correct accents always. "Qué," "cómo," "dónde" — never dropped.
- Ñ not N. "Años," not "anos."
- Opening question and exclamation marks: **required**. "¿Quién es?" — not "Quién es?"
- Em dash (—) for emphasis and interruption. Not double hyphens (--) or hyphens surrounded by spaces ( - ).
- Spanish quotation marks «like these» preferred for blocks; regular "these" acceptable for UI and inline.

### Numbers
- Spell out one through nine in prose. Use figures for 10+.
- Dates: "el 3 de marzo de 2013" in prose. "03/03/2013" only in metadata.
- Years: "en 2013" or "en los años 90," never "en los 90s" (anglicism).
- Decades in full: "los años noventa" OK; "los noventa" OK; "los 90s" avoided.

### Capitalization
- Standard Spanish rules: sentence case for titles, not English title case.
- Genre names: lowercase ("rap," "hip hop," "trap"). Not "Rap," "Hip-Hop," "Trap."
- Album and song titles: follow the artist's canonical capitalization. If the artist wrote "DÍAS DE MAL HUMOR" in caps, keep it.
- Names and proper nouns: exact capitalization as used by the subject. "Lil Supa" (not "LIL SUPA" unless that's how he writes it).

### Hip-hop specific
- "Hip hop" two words, lowercase. Not "HipHop," "Hip-Hop," or "hiphop."
- "Rap" lowercase as genre; capitalize only at sentence start.
- "MC" uppercase. "DJ" uppercase. "Freestyle" lowercase.
- "Beef" — borrowed term, no italics (it's naturalized in Spanish rap vocabulary).
- "Flow," "punchline," "bar," "feature" — borrowed, no italics.
- Venezuelan-specific: "tarima," "libre," "batalla" — lowercase.

### Spanish regional considerations
When writing for pan-Hispanic audiences, avoid terms that have different meanings:
- "Coger" has different connotations across countries. Prefer "tomar" when it works.
- Venezuelan slang is OK in pieces about Venezuelan scenes; explain on first use if not self-evident.

## Naming conventions

### Artist names
- Use the artist's canonical stage name first mention with optional real name in parentheses: "Akapellah (Pedro Eliseo Castillo)."
- After first mention, stage name only.
- Honorifics avoided unless the artist uses them as part of their identity.

### Crew and label names
- Exactly as spelled by the group/label. "Dharius" not "DHARIUS" unless the latter is canonical.
- When in doubt, check the official Instagram bio or Spotify for Artists pages.

### Place names
- Spanish names in Spanish prose: "Nueva York," not "New York."
- Venezuelan cities: "Caracas," "Maracaibo," "Valencia," "Maracay."
- Neighborhoods and sectors: as locally known, italicized on first use if unfamiliar to broader readers.

### Era references
- "Los años noventa" / "los noventa" — both OK, first is more formal.
- "La era del underground" / "la era de las tarimas" — lowercase, descriptive, not fixed terms.

## Sourcing and attribution

### How to cite in prose

- First mention of source: full attribution.
  > "Según una entrevista con Rolling Stone en Español en marzo de 2022…"

- Subsequent mentions: short form.
  > "La misma entrevista reveló que…"

- Multiple sources on same claim:
  > "Confirmado por Rolling Stone en Español (2022), Remezcla (2021), y el sello directamente…"

### How to handle interviews we didn't conduct
- Always attribute: "En una entrevista con [outlet]…"
- Never pass off external interviews as ours.
- When we do our own interviews, attribute explicitly: "En una conversación con Culture Wiki…"

### How to handle primary sources we can't link
- Personal interview not yet published: "Fuente entrevistada por Culture Wiki, abril 2026."
- Off-record or anonymous: "Una fuente cercana a la producción, que pidió anonimato…" — use sparingly.

### How to handle uncertainty
- "Según reportes que no hemos podido verificar independientemente…"
- "Varios reportes indican…"
- Never assert as fact what we cannot source.

## Structural conventions

### Headlines
- Descriptive over clever. "Cómo Canserbero cambió el rap venezolano" beats "El fenómeno que nadie vio venir."
- Length: ≤70 characters when possible (fits in browser tabs, Google results).
- Avoid clickbait entirely — no "no creerás," no "esto cambiará todo."

### Subheadings
- Within an article, use them to structure long pieces. Sentence case.
- Should read as outline — a reader scrolling should get the argument from subheads alone.

### Ledes
- Open with the specific and concrete, not the abstract.
- No "desde tiempos inmemoriales," no "en un mundo donde…"
- A good lede sets up a question the rest of the piece answers.

### Paragraphs
- Vary paragraph length deliberately. Short paragraphs for punch, longer for development.
- One idea per paragraph. When the subject shifts, break.

### Ending
- Stick the landing. Don't restate; advance.
- Avoid "solo el tiempo lo dirá" conclusions. Take a position.

## Bio conventions (for structured bios on entity pages)

### Bio short (50-100 words)
- One paragraph.
- Answers: who, where from, what role, what's most notable.
- Reads like an encyclopedia entry, not a press release.

Example structure:
> [Stage name], nacido [year optional], es un [role] [origen]. [One distinguishing fact or context]. [Most notable work or moment].

### Bio long (300-800 words)
- 3-5 paragraphs.
- Chronological where appropriate; thematic where chronology doesn't serve.
- Sourced throughout.
- Never hagiographic. Acknowledges both accomplishments and difficulties.
- Ends grounded in the present — what they are doing now.

## Dates and timelines

- Dates for career events: most specific verifiable level. "3 de marzo de 2018" > "marzo de 2018" > "2018."
- "Around 2010" or "aproximadamente 2010" acceptable when sourced, but strive for precision.
- "Early 2010s" / "a principios de los 2010" acceptable for loose periodization.
- Never write "ayer," "hoy," "recientemente" — these become stale. Use specific dates.

## UI microcopy conventions

- **Concise, clear, kind.**
- Button labels: verb-led. "Guardar" not "Guardando los cambios." "Corregir" not "Reportar error" (reportar sounds hostile).
- Empty states: what goes here + what to do.
  > "Aún no hemos documentado las colaboraciones de este artista. ¿Conoces alguna? Envíanos una corrección."
- Error messages: what happened + what to try.
  > "No pudimos cargar los datos. Intenta recargar la página."
- Confirmations: gentle, clear.
  > "Artista agregado a favoritos."

### Tu / Usted
Use **tú** throughout. We're addressing peers, not formal strangers. Exception: admin warnings where formality adds seriousness ("Esta acción es permanente").

## Inclusivity and sensitivity

### Gender
- Use the person's preferred pronouns and presentation.
- Don't assume based on stage name.
- When gender is unknown, use their name or their stage name.
- Avoid gendered generic plurals where reasonable ("les artistas" is a style choice some writers make; we permit it but don't require it). Default to the traditional masculine generic unless a specific writer/editor prefers otherwise for the piece.

### Race and ethnicity
- Mention when contextually relevant (e.g., Afro-Venezuelan identity when discussing the artist's work or scene context).
- Avoid when not relevant.

### Class and background
- Respectful, specific. Avoid generic "humble origins" tropes.
- Don't romanticize poverty.
- Don't mock wealth.

### Mental health and addiction
- Discussed only when the artist has made it public or it's directly relevant and well-sourced.
- Language: "luchó con la adicción," "fue tratado por depresión" — not sensational or judgmental.
- Suicide reporting: follow responsible reporting guidelines. No methods, no graphic detail, include context and resources when appropriate.

### Death
- "Falleció" (most neutral). "Murió" also fine.
- Cause of death only when public and contextually relevant. Never speculate.
- Tributes are welcome. Hagiography is not — we write about real, complicated people.

## Common errors to avoid

- "Influenciado por" — prefer "influido por." (Both accepted; "influenciado" is anglicism in origin.)
- "Performance" as noun in prose — prefer "actuación" or "presentación." Acceptable as borrowed term ("performance en vivo"), use sparingly.
- "Mixtape" — naturalized in rap vocabulary, no italics.
- "Samplear" — acceptable Venezuelan rap term. "Sampleó" as past tense.
- Over-use of "prácticamente," "básicamente," "literalmente" — usually cuttable without loss.
- Hedging in factual claims. If you can't say it, cut it. If you can source it, assert it.
