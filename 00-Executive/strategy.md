## Strategic positioning

Culture Wiki sits at the intersection of three markets, each with precedent but none with a dedicated pan-Hispanic offering:

| Market | Dominant English players | Spanish-language equivalent |
|---|---|---|
| Music reference data | Discogs, MusicBrainz, AllMusic | None |
| Discovery & context | Genius, Rate Your Music | None |
| Long-form music journalism | Pitchfork, The Ringer, Complex | Partially: Remezcla, Rolling Stone ES |

The strategic bet: **a single platform that delivers all three in Spanish wins the category**, because no Spanish-speaking audience has a go-to for any of them individually, let alone together.

## Competitive moats

We build five moats sequentially:

1. **Data density.** The more entities and the more connections between them, the more useful the archive becomes. This is the first moat and the hardest to replicate.
2. **Editorial authority.** Serious writing, sourced claims, neutral tone. Earns the trust of press, artists, and institutions.
3. **Community.** Artists who claim their profiles; fans who contribute corrections; journalists who cite us. Network effects.
4. **SEO land grab.** Structured data + consistent publishing captures organic search for every artist, producer, label, venue, and event in the scene. Whoever ranks for "Akapellah discografía" first, wins.
5. **Licensable dataset.** Once the graph is comprehensive, it becomes a commercial asset for labels, academic institutions, and media. This is the last moat and the one that makes the business defensible long-term.

## Go-to-market

### Phase 1 — Quiet build (Months 1-3)
Build the archive without marketing. Focus on depth and completeness of the Venezuelan core. No launch. Private beta to a small circle for feedback.

### Phase 2 — Soft launch (Month 4)
Public URL. Seed content complete for ~50 artists. Organic outreach to Spanish-speaking hip-hop journalists, selected artists, Discord communities. Goal: first 1,000 organic visits, first press mentions.

### Phase 3 — Sustained publishing (Months 5-9)
Consistent content cadence. Weekly editorial piece. Daily data additions. SEO flywheel starts. Partner with 2-3 aligned creators for cross-promotion. Goal: 10k monthly visitors.

### Phase 4 — Commercial activation (Months 10-12)
Memberships open. First sponsorships. First B2B conversations with labels and media. Goal: first $1,000 MRR.

## Competitive risks

- **Genius expands to Latin market seriously.** Mitigation: velocity and specificity. They can't match cultural depth without acquisition.
- **A major label builds an internal version.** Mitigation: independence is the differentiator — we cover everyone, they only care about their roster.
- **An established Latin publication (Remezcla, Rolling Stone ES) launches an archive.** Mitigation: they are editorial-first, not data-first. Data is the moat.
- **A well-funded startup enters with same thesis.** Mitigation: head start + independence + authenticity. Build the network before they can.

## Non-negotiables

- We never take artist money for preferential placement.
- We never take label money to remove criticism.
- We never generate content we can't source.
- We never centralize the archive in a platform we don't control (no Medium, no Substack as primary home, no platform risk).
